Solving e-waste is not as simple as it seems. Recycling, although ideal in theory, is less so in practice. Most e-waste is recycled in nations where labor is cheap and environmental restrictions are low.

Photo by Chien-Min Chung
From Wong et al

As a result, most recycling practices are bad for the environment, the people recycling, and even the industry itself.The chemicals present inside recycled electronics are released into the environment by being burned, dipped into acid, and thrown into rivers.

And, e-waste recycling leads to bootlegged components

These electrical components are extracted from old PCBs, refurbished, and passed off as brand new chips. This can be a major safety and security concern if they are to fail in production.

How can you keep that from happening?

Reduce

There's no better way than to use less of it!

The best way to prevent e-waste build-up is to simply buy less of it. Not only do you reduce the amount of e-waste, but by purchasing less electronics, you reduce your carbon footprint. This means less pollution to the environment, in more ways than one.

Reuse

Don't trash if you can stash

Instead of throwing your electronics away, consider putting it to a different use. Perhaps your desktop can be repurposed into a server, or maybe your television can double as a computer monitor. If it ain't broke, don't throw it away!

E-cycling Programs

There are countless nonprofits that are more than willing to take your "waste!" One such nonprofit is Reboot for Youth, a 501(c)3 organization founded by TJ students dedicated to e-cycling. Reboot for Youth empowers children in need with the computers you donate!

Don't have a computer or accessory to donate?

If you live locally in Fairfax County, you're in luck! Fairfax County also runs an e-cycling program, and you can e-cycle any household electronics for free. They are all week long, and are the perfect solution for residents in the County.

Recycle

And if there's no other way...

Last but not least, recycling your old electronics can still be beneficial to the environment. Although recycling evidently pollutes the environment, it remains better than sending your electronics off to a landfill. When you recycle electronics, they do not get melted down into liquid metals as you might expect. Instead, they get stripped of their reusable components.
